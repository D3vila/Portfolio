About Me

My story begins in the Grand Canyon, Arizona. Ever since I was young, I’ve been drawn to gaming and computers. My first video game system was a Nintendo Game Boy. As a kid, I was fascinated by how such a small device could hold an entire world inside it. As my brother and I got older, we’d spend hours playing games, searching for cheat codes (like A-B-A-C-A-B-B for blood mode in Mortal Kombat on the Sega Genesis), and tinkering with settings to see how we could bend the rules of the game.

In high school, I was reintroduced to computer programming through a basic computer class. I vividly remember how much I enjoyed the HTML section. I loved the ability to shape and customize a webpage exactly how I envisioned it. That passion deepened in college, where I built my first website entirely in HTML. It was a small project, but it was the moment I realized that programming was something I truly loved.

Today, I live in Phoenix, Arizona with my amazing wife, 2 kiddos, and our three dogs. When COVID hit, it gave me the chance to slow down and finally pursue the passion I’d carried with me for years. There’s something incredibly satisfying about turning an idea into a tangible product. Coding has become my outlet for creativity and problem-solving. Looking back on my journey—from my first console.log("Hello World") to building full-stack web applications—I’m proud of how far I’ve come. I'm excited to see where this path will take me next.
